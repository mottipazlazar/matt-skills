# Platform-Specific Rules for Land Listings

---

## LandSearch.com - Strict Compliance Rules

LandSearch has the most restrictive content policy. Title and description must follow:

### Prohibited Content (in title and description)
- No pricing, payments, down payment, financing terms
- No taxes, HOA fees, MRID fees
- No content implying payments or financial obligations
- No promotional or persuasive language
- No contact info, links, HTML, or calls to action
- No generic or duplicate content
- No guarantees or money-back language

### Required
- Factual, objective wording only
- Proper grammar and clear, neutral sentences
- All details must match the property accurately
- Technical and location details are allowed: coordinates, parcel number, zoning,
  utilities, taxes, access, lot size, proximity to towns and features

### Photo Rules
- Cover must be an exterior photo of the actual property
- No stock or AI images
- No overlays, captions, borders
- Map screenshots should not replace the cover photo

### Creative Approach - Push to the Edge

LandSearch says factual and objective. It does NOT say boring. The goal is to write
the most engaging, descriptive, and immersive listing possible WITHOUT breaking any
compliance rules.

**What you CAN do (and should):**
- Use rich, descriptive language about the land, terrain, surroundings, and area character
- Paint a picture of the setting - what the street feels like, what the area looks like
- Describe the lifestyle the area is known for (factual - what people actually do there)
- Mention what the area attracts (families, retirees, outdoor enthusiasts) as a factual
  observation, not a sales pitch
- Use varied sentence length and rhythm - the Slippery Slide still applies to structure
- Write longer descriptions - 5-7 paragraphs of rich, factual storytelling
- Describe what the zoning and county rules enable (2 dwellings, compound layout, etc.)
  as factual possibilities, not sales hooks
- Include sensory details grounded in fact (wooded terrain, dirt road character, lake proximity)

**What you CANNOT do:**
- Use urgency, scarcity, or FOMO language
- Include any pricing, financing, or payment references
- Add CTAs, contact info, or guarantees
- Use direct persuasion ("you should buy this", "do not miss this")
- Include HOA fees, MRID fees, or tax amounts in the text

**The line:** You can say "The area is known for its low cost of living and growing
community of families." You cannot say "This is a deal you do not want to miss."
You can say "The county allows two separate dwellings." You cannot say "Imagine
building your dream home and a guest house."

### How to Convert an Existing Ad to LandSearch

1. Strip all pricing, financing, payment, and guarantee language
2. Remove all CTAs and contact info
3. Replace persuasive/emotional language with descriptive/factual equivalents
4. Keep factual property details: location, size, zoning, utilities, access, nearby features
5. Rewrite in a rich, descriptive, factual tone - push the creative edge without breaking rules
6. Generate a new compliant title (factual but specific and interesting)
7. Aim for 5-7 paragraphs - longer and more engaging than a basic spec sheet

## Landmodo

### Buckets (Buyer-Intent Categories)

For each listing, suggest which buckets to select:

1. **Live on today** - Property is ready for immediate use (utilities nearby, road access,
   buildable, habitable area)
2. **Buy today for future** - Good investment/hold property (growing area, below market,
   development potential)
3. **Recreational** - Suited for camping, hunting, off-roading, outdoor activities
4. **Land Banking** - Pure investment play (appreciation potential, emerging area)

Most properties fit 1-2 buckets. Suggest with reasoning based on property details.

### Copy Rules
- Full marketing copy allowed
- Pricing and financing terms allowed
- CTAs allowed
- Emotional/persuasive language allowed
- Use the full Hormozi + Slippery Slide approach

## Facebook Marketplace

### Adaptation Rules
- Shorter copy - Facebook users scroll fast
- Lead with the hook and price
- Use the avatar's emotional language
- Include the key 2-3 selling points
- Clear CTA (message, call, or link)
- No long paragraphs - break everything up
- Price anchoring works well here (was $X, now $Y)

### Format
- Title: short, punchy, includes price or "Owner Financing"
- Description: 3-4 short paragraphs max
- Always include location, size, zoning, price

## General Cross-Platform Rules

- All titles and copy always in English regardless of conversation language
- No land improvements offered - buyers are responsible
- Listings mention only available use and zoning
- Maintain brand consistency across all platforms
- Sign off as "Dealflow"
- No em dashes - hyphens with spaces only
- HTML tags returned inside code blocks so they are visible and copyable
- Line spacing (br br) and light emphasis (b, strong) preferred for readability
